MiTraveler 7D-1A Review

General Specifications

The MiTraveler 7D-1A is an Android tablet, running Android 4.0.4 (Ice Cream Sandwich).

The tablet sports an AllWinner Boxchip A13, clocked at 1.00 GHz (Gigahertz).

It has a 0.3-megapixel camera for video calling. Although I don't think it's that great. It renders max in VGA anyway.

Performance

The performance depends on what you download on it and use the tablet for. Me? I use it to check my email.

Checking my email doesn't take that much RAM, especially due to the tablet only having 512 megabytes of it.

You could try and find AllWinner A13 custom kernels, but I doubt there'd be anything because of the obscurity of the device. AllWinner A13 XDA Forum Threads

Touchscreen

The tablet has a 7 inch touchscreen, 5 points. You can test this in a touchscreen test app.

I do find it needs a little bit more pressure than usual, but I might just be skinny.

Display

The display isn't that great of a display. It's an 800x480 display with (I estimate...) 16 million colors.

It's clocked at 60 Hz aswell.

The display is fine for reading ebooks and emails, along with basic games such as Angry Birds or Fruit Ninja.

Storage

Even for low expectations, the storage is disappointing. It's only 8 gigabytes. So beware of storage runouts, or just buy a 32GB MicroSD Card.

But it's good enough storage for writing small documents. DEFINITELY DON'T TRY MAKING PROGRAMS ON THIS.

Speaker

The speaker is definitely not an all-winner (hah, see what I did there?), but it does have a 3.5mm headphone jack. So you can connect external ear/headphones.

But, its quiet enough to use in the middle of the night so you dont wake up your parents. Heheh.

Battery Life

Battery life unfortunately doesn't do a justice. At a whopping 1-2 hours of battery life, you'll mostly be having this handset plugged in at all times.

Sad to say; pretty much no redeeming qualities of the battery life.

Finally, the Physical Design

All around the tablet is made of cheap material. Although there are redeeming qualities such as a plastic, textured grip along the faux-wooden rear end.

The screen is made of plastic though so don't drop it lol

Conclusion

In conclusion, the MiTraveler is an entry-level device, with very low specifications.

So if you want something cheap and low end to check your email on or play very light games, this is the device for you.
